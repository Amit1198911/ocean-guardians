Ocean Awareness

Take a Dive

The ocean is a vast and mysterious world that covers over 70% of the Earth's surface. It is home to a diverse range of life forms, from the tiniest plankton to the largest whales. Understanding and preserving this incredible biodiversity is crucial for the health of our planet.However, this delicate beauty is under threat due to various human activities that are causing unprecedented levels of pollution

Impact Of Pollution on Marine Life

  • Millions of tons of plastic waste end up in the ocean each year, harming marine animals that ingest or become entangled in plastic debris.
  • Industrial runoff, agricultural chemicals, and oil spills introduce toxic substances into marine environments, poisoning marine life and disrupting ecosystems.

    Ocean Health is Critical for Human Survival

    Oceans produce at least 50% of the planet's oxygen, which is essential for human life. They also absorb about 30% of the carbon dioxide produced by humans, buffering the impacts of global warming

    Coral Reefs are Vital and Vulnerable

    Coral reefs support a vast number of marine species and provide critical ecosystem services, such as coastal protection and tourism revenue. They are highly sensitive to changes in temperature and acidity, making them particularly vulnerable to climate change.

    Plastic Pollution is a Major Threat

    Approximately 8 million tons of plastic enter the oceans each year, causing harm to marine life and ecosystems. This pollution can result in the ingestion and entanglement of marine animals, and microplastics can enter the human food chain through seafood.

    Deep Sea Universe

    More than 80% of the ocean remains unexplored. The deep sea holds potential for new scientific discoveries, including new species and medical compounds, but it is also vulnerable to human exploitation and pollution.
